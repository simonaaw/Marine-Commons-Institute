The Initiative

The Marine AI Consortium (MAC) is a global alliance that unites AI technology with ocean research to develop open-source tools and best practices for AI-powered marine biodiversity monitoring.


Based at UC San Diego's Scripps Institution of Oceanography, MAC creates a student-focused, inclusive community where educators, students, and scientists work together to empower "citizen scientists" ‚Äì everyday coastal stewards armed with low-cost technology ‚Äì to protect coastal ecosystems and marine life.

Our Vision

To establish open-source standards and best practices for AI-powered marine biomonitoring - enabling any coastal steward to deploy low-cost, validated sensors in local waters and get reliable data, even without advanced laboratories.


"Precision Medicine" for the Ocean - tailored monitoring for each bay, estuary, and reef.

Climate & Biodiversity Crisis

Our oceans are experiencing unprecedented change. Rising temperatures, acidification, and deoxygenation are fundamentally altering marine ecosystems at rates never seen in human history.

"Coral reefs have declined by 50% in just 30 years"

Fish populations are migrating poleward, food webs are collapsing, and entire ecosystems face extinction. The ocean's capacity to support life and provide essential services to humanity hangs in the balance.

Monitoring Limitations

Traditional marine research methods like trawling and diving surveys are expensive, time-intensive, and often invasive to the very ecosystems we're trying to protect.

"A single research vessel costs $50,000+ per day"

These approaches miss cryptic species, can't access remote locations, and provide only snapshots in time. Meanwhile, the ocean's biodiversity changes faster than we can track it.

üìä

Critical Data Gaps

Without comprehensive monitoring, we're flying blind on conservation decisions. Data gaps prevent early detection of threats, hinder policy development, and limit our ability to measure conservation success.

"Less than 5% of ocean biodiversity is regularly monitored"

This information void undermines global commitments like the Global Biodiversity Framework and leaves coastal communities vulnerable to environmental surprises.

Our Approach & What We Do

MAC transforms marine biodiversity monitoring through innovative technology and community engagement. We combine cutting-edge environmental DNA analysis with artificial intelligence to democratize ocean science and empower the next generation of coastal stewards.

Environmental DNA (eDNA)

Detect multiple marine species from a single water sample, including cryptic, migratory, and rare organisms - without direct observation or capture. This revolutionary approach provides comprehensive biodiversity insights from minimal sampling effort.

ü§ñ

AI-Powered Analysis

Artificial intelligence rapidly analyzes large volumes of eDNA data, identifying species patterns, flagging anomalies, and predicting ecological trends. Our cloud-based platform makes advanced analysis accessible to students and educators worldwide.

Citizen Science Network

Democratize biodiversity tracking by empowering communities to participate in data collection using accessible, low-cost technology. Build a global network of citizen scientists contributing to marine conservation.
