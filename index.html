Discover Our Initiative

The Initiative

The Marine AI Consortium (MAC) is a global alliance that unites AI technology with ocean research to develop open-source tools and best practices for AI-powered marine biodiversity monitoring.


Based at UC San Diego's Scripps Institution of Oceanography, MAC creates a student-focused, inclusive community where educators, students, and scientists work together to empower "citizen scientists" – everyday coastal stewards armed with low-cost technology – to protect coastal ecosystems and marine life.

Our Vision

To establish open-source standards and best practices for AI-powered marine biomonitoring - enabling any coastal steward to deploy low-cost, validated sensors in local waters and get reliable data, even without advanced laboratories.


"Precision Medicine" for the Ocean - tailored monitoring for each bay, estuary, and reef.

Climate & Biodiversity Crisis

Marine biodiversity faces unprecedented threats from climate change, pollution, and habitat destruction. Ocean warming, acidification, and deoxygenation are altering food webs and ecosystem functions at rates never seen before. Coral reefs are bleaching at alarming rates, fish populations are declining, and entire ecosystems are shifting due to warming waters. The ocean's capacity to provide vital services for society and economies is now nearing a tipping point.

Monitoring Limitations

Traditional marine monitoring methods like trawling, visual surveys by divers, and tagging individual animals remain challenging and expensive. These techniques are often time-consuming, costly to deploy, and difficult to implement in remote or hard-to-access regions. Furthermore, traditional methods may fail to capture the full complexity of marine ecosystems, particularly for elusive or microscopic species, or for critical habitats like seagrass meadows and mangroves.

Critical Data Gaps

Without comprehensive, scalable monitoring systems, it becomes difficult to assess the effectiveness of conservation interventions or to detect early warning signs of emerging environmental threats. Data gaps hinder national conservation planning, enforcement capabilities, and reporting on global biodiversity commitments like the Global Biodiversity Framework and Sustainable Development Goals. This lack of information prevents informed decision-making at both local and international levels.

Our Approach & What We Do

MAC transforms marine biodiversity monitoring through innovative technology and community engagement. We combine cutting-edge environmental DNA analysis with artificial intelligence to democratize ocean science and empower the next generation of coastal stewards.

🧬

Environmental DNA (eDNA)

Detect multiple marine species from a single water sample, including cryptic, migratory, and rare organisms - without direct observation or capture. This revolutionary approach provides comprehensive biodiversity insights from minimal sampling effort.

🤖

AI-Powered Analysis

Artificial intelligence rapidly analyzes large volumes of eDNA data, identifying species patterns, flagging anomalies, and predicting ecological trends. Our cloud-based platform makes advanced analysis accessible to students and educators worldwide.

🌍

Citizen Science Network

Democratize biodiversity tracking by empowering communities to participate in data collection using accessible, low-cost technology. Build a global network of citizen scientists contributing to marine conservation.

📚

Educational Curriculum

Comprehensive, open-source program guiding students through the full cycle of modern ocean biomonitoring - from sample collection to data visualization and scientific communication.

🛠️

Technology Development

Develop and refine portable field kits combining eDNA sampling, AI processing, and cloud-based analysis platforms. Make cutting-edge marine science tools accessible and affordable.

🔓

Open-Source Standards

Establish transparent protocols and software tools that ensure reliability, reproducibility, and accessibility across diverse settings. All data and methods are openly shared for global benefit.

Why This Approach Works

Cost-Effective & Scalable

Less expensive than traditional monitoring systems, ideal for widespread deployment with limited conservation budgets. Perfect for schools, NGOs, and developing nations.

Accessible & Portable

Fully portable and operational off-grid, making it ideal for island states, rural fishing villages, and areas with weak infrastructure.

Comprehensive & Rapid

Detects species often missed by traditional methods while enabling near real-time detection of pollution, invasive species, and habitat changes.

Globally Connected

Contributes to global biodiversity databases and supports international reporting frameworks, fostering regional cooperation and knowledge sharing.

Student Workflow: From Sample to Insight

Our curriculum transforms students into citizen scientist ocean stewards through hands-on experience

1

Field Collection

Students collect water samples containing eDNA from local waters using portable field kits

2

Lab Processing

Filter water and extract DNA with simple kits, guided by mentors for proper technique

3

DNA Sequencing

Use handheld Oxford Nanopore sequencer to read DNA samples in real-time

4

Cloud Analysis

Upload data to UCSD-hosted cloud lab for AI-powered species identification

5

Data Visualization

Create biodiversity dashboards and mini-papers to share findings publicly

Field Kit Components

NVIDIA Jetson

Mini AI computer serving as the kit's brain, running AI models and processing sensor data on-site

Oxford Nanopore Sequencer

Handheld DNA sequencer (e.g. MinION) for real-time genetic analysis in the field

Starlink Uplink

Portable satellite internet unit providing off-grid connectivity for immediate data upload

UCSD Cloud Lab

JupyterHub platform giving students personal notebook workspaces for analysis
